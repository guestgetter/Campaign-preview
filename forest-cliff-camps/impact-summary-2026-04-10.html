The Story in One Page

Forest Cliff's 2026 Meta advertising program launched on January 20, 2026. Over the twelve weeks since, it has delivered measurable, traceable, profitable growth — and built a foundation that will continue to compound for years.

$8,808 spent has been directly traced to 105 new households (~150 new campers) registering through CampBrain, generating roughly $83,200 in Year-1 revenue at an average cost of $59 per camper acquired. That's a 9.4× return on ad spend in Year 1 alone — and because Forest Cliff's own data shows the average returning family attends for 3.56 years, the projected 5-year value of those same 105 families is between $223,000 and $416,000, depending on retention.

For context, the 2025 Facebook Ads benchmark for the Education industry is roughly $28 per lead and an implied $280 per customer acquired. Forest Cliff is running at $8 per lead and $59 per camper — about 3.4× more efficient than the published industry average. This isn't an accident: it reflects a real product (87 years of trust, OCA accreditation, 4.8 stars across 368 Google reviews), proper conversion tracking infrastructure, and weekly active optimization.

Beyond direct registrations, the program has built three durable assets that belong to Forest Cliff and persist after this report:

  • A 902-person warm lead pipeline in HighLevel — families who raised their hand but haven't yet converted, ready for follow-up at zero additional ad cost.
  • A 20,000-person engaged video viewer audience on Meta — parents who watched at least 25% of a Forest Cliff video ad in the last 90 days.
  • Two 1% Lookalike audiences totaling ~540,000 Canadian parents modeled on the families already converting — ready to deploy in cold prospecting.
