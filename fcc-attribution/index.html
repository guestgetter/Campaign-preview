The bottom line

92 households can be traced from a Meta lead form to a CampBrain registration — matched by email, phone, and surname. That is $41,400 in provable ad-driven revenue on $6,286 in ad spend (all-time, per Meta Ads Manager) = 6.6× ROAS on direct attribution alone.

GA4 shows an additional layer of influence: direct traffic to fccamps.ca rose 34% after ads launched, which suggests many families saw ads and registered without ever filling out a lead form. True ROAS is higher than 6.6× — the 92 matches are the floor.

Layer 4

What we can’t measure (but know exists)

  • Phone calls — Parents who saw an ad and called the camp directly.
  • Word of mouth — “Have you heard about Forest Cliff? I saw this ad…”
  • Spouse registration with no digital trail — One parent sees the ad, discusses with partner; partner registers from a different device without UTMs.
  • Multi-touch over weeks — Saw the ad in January, registered in March.
  • Church / community amplification — Referrers include westparkchurch.ca, calvaryburlington.ca, cbcilderton.ca; ad awareness likely accelerated network referrals.

The math: 1,770 households registered. 790 ever touched a GHL lead form. That means 980 households (55%) registered without filling out any lead form — they found Forest Cliff through other paths, many plausibly influenced by seeing ads in Facebook/Instagram.

Playbook

Acceleration — convert more, faster

1
Immediate lead follow-up sequence ($0 ad spend)

The biggest lever. 650 leads in HighLevel with email + phone — they raised their hand. Most never heard back.

Week 1 — Hot leads (0–14 days, 213 leads)

  • Day 0 (within hours): SMS — “Hi [First Name], thanks for your interest in Forest Cliff! Here’s the Parent Handbook: [link]. Any questions? Just reply here.”
  • Day 1: Email — Parent Handbook PDF + session dates + pricing overview.
  • Day 3: SMS — “Quick question — Day Camp or Overnight for [child]? Happy to help you pick the right session.”
  • Day 5: Email — Social proof: what parents said last summer + Google reviews + gallery link.
  • Day 7: SMS — Urgency: “[Session] is ~80% full — wanted you to have a chance before it fills.”

Weeks 2–3 — Warm (15–30 days, 223 leads)

  • Reactivation email: “Still thinking about camp? 3 things parents wish they knew…” (safety, homesickness, what’s included).
  • SMS with a specific session recommendation using grade-level tags.
  • Final email: “Holding a spot for [child] — registration closes [date].”

Week 4+ — Cold (31–60 days, 202 leads)

  • One email: “Summer 2026 spots going fast — 7 sessions already full.”
  • If they click but don’t register → trigger retarget sequence.

Expected conversion: 10–20% hot, 5–10% warm, 2–5% cold → 65–130 additional registrations = $29,250–$58,500 revenue at $0 incremental ad spend.

2
Scale retarget ($100–150/day)

Retarget is efficient (~$13.31/reg, ~73% lead-to-reg). Now ~$72/day. Test $100/day, then $150 if frequency stays <3.5. At $100/day ~7.5 regs/day from retarget; ~$3k/mo → ~225 regs/mo projection. Watch frequency (2.47 now).

3
Lookalike from converters

This has been recommended in every monitor since March 20 and still hasn’t been done. Upload the 92 matched registrant emails as a Custom Audience seed; create a 1% Lookalike — parents who look like people who actually registered, not just filled out a form. Test in a new ad set in cold campaigns at $25–50/day. Expect 20–40% better CPL than current cold targeting because the seed is registration-qualified.

4
Retarget site visitors (no lead form)

GA4 shows 1,228 paid sessions hit the website — most browsed but didn’t fill a lead form or register; they’re already in the pixel pool. Keep Website Visitors 30d active in retarget. Add Visited registration page but didn’t complete — these are the highest-intent people. Ads: urgency (“Sessions filling fast”) + social proof.

5
Leverage the CampBrain email list

1,770 registered households = 3,030 parent emails.

  • Custom audience exclusion — Stop ads to people who already registered; cuts waste and lifts efficiency.
  • Lookalike seed — 1% LAL from 3,030 paying parents is the strongest cold seed available (not lead-form fillers).
  • Referral campaign — Email registered families: “Know a family who’d love camp? Share this — they’ll get $X off.” Word-of-mouth from registered families is the #1 driver of camp registrations.
6
Phone outreach (high-intent)

628 unconverted leads have phone numbers. Prioritize 49 tagged overnight-camp-interest and 35 Grade 7–9 (Jr. High — limited spots): personal staff call, session urgency, answer questions. 84 leads × 20% conversion → 17 registrations ≈ $7,650 revenue. Camp staff voice beats automation for high-value regs.
