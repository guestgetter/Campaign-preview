The bottom line

36 households can be directly traced: Meta lead form → GHL → CampBrain registration. That is the hard, match-based floor.

GA4 shows ads influenced roughly 284 households through paid clicks plus organic and direct lift — about $128,000 in estimated revenue. The true story sits between the provable 36 and that fuller picture.

Layer 4

What we cannot measure (but know exists)

  • Phone calls — Parents who saw an ad and called the camp directly.
  • Word of mouth — “I saw this ad about Forest Cliff…”
  • Spouse forwarding — One parent sees the ad; the other registers with a different email.
  • Multi-touch — Saw the ad in January; registered in March after many touchpoints.
  • Church / community amplification — Referrals often flow through churches; ad awareness can accelerate that network.
Appendix

Data sources & methodology

Sources

  1. CampBrain CSV — 1,770 households, 3,030 parent emails, 2,932 phones (2026 registrations).
  2. HighLevel CRM — 790 contacts (706 from Facebook ads, 84 from other).
  3. GA4 (property 477599729) — Traffic by source/medium, weekly.
  4. Meta Ads API — Campaign spend and performance.

Methodology notes

  • Direct attribution: Email + phone match between GHL and CampBrain parents.
  • Traffic influence: Pre-ad baseline (Jan 1 – 18) vs. ad-period weekly averages.
  • Session → registration: Blended 8.1% across sources; new visitors likely convert higher.
  • Value: $450 average registration (conservative; overnight camps often $500–729).
