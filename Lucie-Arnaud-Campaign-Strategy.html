The Core Insight

Every restaurant in the city runs "special events." Almost none of them have a genuine, emotionally resonant, time-boxed reason for urgency. Lucie does. The strategy is simple: make people feel the weight of what's ending so they can't afford to miss it — then immediately reassure them that Lucie's story continues.

The Big Marketing Idea

The Last Table with Chef Arnaud

After 3 years shaping one of the city's most respected kitchens, Chef Arnaud Bloquel is returning to France. His final service is May 2. This is a once-in-a-lifetime dining moment — the kind of thing people tell stories about.

"You don't get a second chance to experience something that's ending. This is the table you'll wish you'd booked."

Big Promise: Experience the food that defined Lucie — before it's gone forever.
Unique Mechanism: Two curated farewell experiences (Souvenirs Tasting + Pithivier Experience) — not just dinner.
Unique Sales Argument: This isn't a marketing event. This is a real chef, leaving a real kitchen, for the last time. Natural urgency. Natural scarcity.

Strategic Positioning

The Messaging Tightrope

Yannick was clear: we honor Arnaud without over-promoting someone leaving, and we make it crystal clear that Lucie isn't closing. Here's the framework that threads that needle.

The Two-Story Architecture

Every piece of content tells two stories simultaneously. The foreground story is about the farewell experience. The background story is about Lucie's exciting evolution. Neither exists without the other.

The ratio shifts over time: March/April is 80% farewell, 20% future. Post-May 2, it flips to 20% gratitude, 80% rebirth.

Always Say

  • ✓ "Lucie's final tasting menu with Chef Arnaud"
  • ✓ "A chapter is closing — a new one begins"
  • ✓ "Lucie evolves" / "Lucie continues"
  • ✓ "Experience the food that built Lucie"
  • ✓ "Your last chance to..." (specific experience)

Never Say

  • ✗ "Lucie is closing"
  • ✗ "Going out of business"
  • ✗ "Last chance to eat at Lucie"
  • ✗ Over-personalizing around Arnaud (it's about the brand)
  • ✗ Dramatic / desperate urgency language
Tone Reference: "Elegant FOMO"

The tone should feel like a letter from a trusted friend — not a countdown timer on a sales page. Think: the quiet confidence of someone who knows the value of what they're offering. The urgency is inherent in the situation. We don't manufacture it. We simply remind people it's real.

Campaign Architecture

The Three-Phase Engine

We're running a compressed campaign with natural urgency built in. No need to manufacture scarcity — we just need to make sure people know it exists.

Phase 01 — March 5–14

The Announcement Wave

Launch day is March 5. Seed the news. Build the waitlist. Drive to the landing page immediately.

  • March 5: Landing page goes live + Announcement email fires — single-purpose conversion page + "Dear Friends of Lucie" letter adapted for email, sent same day
  • March 5: Social announcement — Organic post on Lucie's Instagram + Facebook with the photo of Arnaud & Yannick
  • March 6-7: Paid amplification — Boost the announcement post to lookalike audiences + past engagement audiences
  • March 6-7: PR outreach — Pitch to Toronto food media: "Chef Arnaud Bloquel departs Restaurant Lucie after 3 years"
  • March 7-10: Video capture — Film the iPhone-style Arnaud interview (see Video section)
Phase 02 — March 15 – April 20

The Urgency Engine

Drive continuous bookings through content, ads, and retargeting. The menu is live. The clock is ticking.

  • Weekly video drops — Behind-the-scenes kitchen content, Arnaud preparing signature dishes
  • Meta ad campaigns — Cold traffic (video views) + warm retargeting (booking conversion)
  • Guest testimonial collection — Capture reactions from diners experiencing the farewell menu
  • "Dish Spotlight" content series — Each week, spotlight a signature dish from the Souvenirs menu with its story
  • Email drip sequence — 2x/week to Lucie's database with availability updates and content
  • Partnership activations — Cross-promote with wine partners, local food influencers
Phase 03 — April 21 – May 2

The Final Count

Last 12 days. Maximum intensity. Every remaining seat is precious.

  • "Final Tables" campaign — Direct conversion ads with remaining availability dates
  • Social countdown — "X services remaining" organic content
  • Email urgency sequence — "Final weekend" and "Last night" emails
  • Capture the final service — Video/photo documentation of May 2 for transition content
  • Transition teaser — Begin hinting at Lucie's next chapter to maintain momentum

Video Content Strategy

The Arnaud Interview — Campaign Fuel

This single video becomes the foundation of the entire paid media campaign. Simple iPhone capture. Human. Real. No production crew needed.

🎬

The Hero Video (60-90 sec)

Sit-down with Arnaud in the kitchen. Natural light. iPhone on a tripod. Ask him 3-4 questions. This becomes the cold traffic ad that builds the entire retargeting funnel.

🔪

Kitchen B-Roll (15-30 sec clips)

Arnaud plating dishes, kitchen in motion, the pass during service. These become Reels, Story content, and ad creative variations. Capture 20-30 minutes of raw footage.

💬

Guest Reactions (15 sec each)

As the farewell menu launches, capture genuine guest reactions after their meal. "How was it?" — short, authentic, unscripted. These become retargeting testimonial ads.

📸

The Dish Series (10-15 sec each)

One video per signature dish on the Souvenirs menu. Close-up plating. Arnaud narrates the story behind each dish in a sentence or two. Weekly drops on social.

Arnaud Interview — Suggested Questions

# Question Purpose
1 "When you first walked into Lucie's kitchen, what did you feel?" Origin story — emotional anchor
2 "Which dish on the Souvenirs menu means the most to you, and why?" Product spotlight — drives interest in the specific experience
3 "What do you want people to remember about this chapter of Lucie?" Legacy framing — reinforces the "closing a chapter" narrative
4 "What would you say to someone who's been meaning to come but hasn't yet?" Direct CTA from Arnaud himself — this becomes the ad hook
Why This Works on Meta

Person-centered video content consistently outperforms polished brand creative on Meta by 2-3x. The algorithm rewards human faces, real emotion, and authentic moments. This interview builds a 25%+ viewer retargeting audience that becomes the warm layer of the ad funnel — the people who've already connected with Arnaud's story and just need the booking nudge.

Retargeting Architecture

The Booking Loop

Nobody books a $200+ dinner on the first touch. The retargeting system turns "that looks amazing" into "I just reserved a table" — through sequenced content that builds desire and collapses hesitation.

Audience Segment Trigger Creative Window
Tier 1 Video Viewers Watched 25%+ of Arnaud video Carousel: "5 Dishes You Can Only Try Until May 2" → Landing page 7 days
Tier 2 Engaged Non-Visitors Liked/commented/saved but didn't visit site Guest testimonial video + "What you'll experience" copy 14 days
Tier 3 Landing Page Visitors Visited landing page but didn't click Reserve "Tables for [specific week] are filling" — urgency with specificity 7 days
Tier 4 Reserve Button Clickers Clicked Reserve but didn't complete booking "Your table is waiting" — strongest urgency, simplest message 3 days
Exclusion Audiences (Critical)

Always exclude: People who have already booked (sync with reservation system), people who are in the process of booking (24-hour window), and staff/team members. This prevents wasted spend and annoying your best customers.

Success Metrics

How We Know It's Working

The north star is simple: every service from March 16 to May 2 is fully booked. Here's how we track progress toward that.

📊

Primary KPI

Reservation conversion rate — Landing page visitors → completed bookings. Target: 8-12% (high-intent traffic from retargeting should push this above industry benchmarks).

👁️

Awareness KPIs

Video ThruPlay rate (target: 25%+), 25% video viewer audience size (target: 10,000+ in first 2 weeks), organic reach on announcement post.

🔥

Engagement KPIs

Landing page sessions, time on page (target: 45+ sec), CTA click rate (target: 15%+), email open rate (target: 35%+ — this audience cares).

💰

Revenue KPI

Booking pace — Track weekly against total available covers. Goal: 70% booked by April 15. 95%+ booked by April 28. Waitlisted for final weekend.

Weekly Check-In Cadence

Every Monday: review booking pace, ad spend vs. ROAS, creative fatigue indicators, email engagement. Adjust budget allocation between cold/warm/hot campaigns based on where the bottleneck is. If bookings are strong, shift budget to cold traffic to build the database for the Lucie relaunch.
