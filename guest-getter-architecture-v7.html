Why this is hard for anyone else to copy

Year-over-year data

A new vendor walking in starts from zero. Our retention agent already knows three years of guest patterns.

Cross-client know-how

What worked for a smash burger chain in Montreal improves what we do for a BBQ spot in Toronto. Solo agencies can't.

Industry benchmarks

Anonymized, aggregated patterns across every restaurant we run. What "normal" retention looks like for a smash burger chain. What CPGA looks like in BBQ. No vendor or consultant has this.

Frictionless switching

If they want out, we transfer it. No threats. No drama. The easier you make it to leave, the fewer people leave.

Step four

What makes this work for us.

Templates carry the load.

Every new client gets the same starting point. A template for the website. A standard schema for the Brain. A library of agent prompts. Client #4 takes a fraction of the time client #1 took.

We host it. They can take it.

Everything runs under our roof so we can move fast and share what works across every client. If someone wants out, we transfer the Brain. The easier you make it to leave, the fewer people leave.

The Brain stays invisible.

What gets sold is regulars who come back, slow weeks that have answers, decisions that stop being guesses. The Brain is what makes those wins possible. The wins are what we talk about.

Each month delivers a felt win.

If a month goes by where the owner can't point at one thing that's clearly better, the engagement is broken. The rollout is designed so every month produces a moment they'd describe to a friend.
