The 45-Day Timeline

Three phases. Each builds on the last. We don't move to the next phase until the current one is solid. A lot of the groundwork is already done from the past year — this timeline is about activating it, not building from scratch. Manual work first. We're too junior for automation — Wassim's words, and he's right.

Phase 01
Foundation
Days 1–15
Build the infrastructure. Get the data. Know your customers.
  • Import UEat database into CRM — Export all customers. Import with proper tags: source, location, last order date. Answer: How many customers do we have?
  • Import offer opt-ins — Merge existing lead capture contacts from ads and offers.
  • Tag and segment the database — By source (UEat, website, offer, third-party), order frequency, location (Masson/Wellington), last order date.
  • Establish baseline metrics — Total customers, ordered more than once, lapsed (30+ days). Fill in the scorecard starting point.
  • Finalize the executive scorecard — Agree on 5-6 numbers we track, where data comes from, and review cadence.
  • Lock Joyo positioning — 'Joyo is the ___ burger in Montreal.' This drives every ad, every message, every piece of content.
  • Audit both Google Business Profiles — Masson + Wellington. Ensure review agents are active.
  • Set up tracking — UTMs on all links, QR code tracking, promo code system for insert cards.
Exit Criteria: Database imported and segmented. Scorecard populated with baseline numbers. Positioning locked.
Phase 02
Activation
Days 16–30
Turn on the machine. First messages go live. Order Direct campaign launches.
  • Design and print insert cards — For every third-party delivery bag. QR code to joyoburger.com with UTM tracking. Incentive: free fries or 10% off first direct order.
  • Deploy insert cards at both locations — Staff trained: 'Put one in every delivery bag.' No exceptions.
  • Build post-order email sequence — Same-day thank you + nudge (Day 5-7) + winback (Day 21-30). Every email has a specific purpose.
  • Launch first two DNA messages — From the Message Playbook: 'We Grind Our Own Beef Every Day' + one more. Material gathered, produced, deployed as paid ads.
  • Plan first content shoot — Kitchen/process shoot covers grinding, hand-cut fries, transparency. Schedule, shot list, who's filming.
  • Launch 'Order Direct' retargeting ads — Target warm audiences (video viewers, website visitors, social engagers) with direct order messaging.
  • Send first intentional email to database — Not a generic newsletter. A specific message with a specific purpose. Something that shows we know what we're doing.
  • Website-only incentive live — Something ONLY available when ordering direct. Free fries, loyalty points, or lower prices.
Exit Criteria: Insert cards in bags. Post-order sequence live. First DNA content running as ads. Direct order incentive active.
Phase 03
Acceleration
Days 31–45
Read the data. Optimize. Double down on what's working.
  • Review scorecard trends — Are website orders trending up? Is the database growing? What's the insert card conversion rate?
  • Launch next two DNA messages — Based on Phase 2 performance. Rotate message angles.
  • Build winback sequence — For lapsed customers (30+ days). Specific offer, specific message: 'It's been a while. Here's something for you.'
  • Optimize Order Direct ads — Kill underperformers. Scale what's converting. Test new angles.
  • Plan first LTO or event — Something that gives the database a reason to order NOW. Not just 'hey we exist' — a specific, time-bound reason.
  • Second content shoot — People/team focus: Meet the Crew, Wassim's story, the Joy philosophy.
  • Review + optimize email sequences — Open rates, click rates, actual orders driven. What subject lines work? What messaging converts?
  • Set 90-day targets — Based on 45-day data, project forward. What's realistic? What needs to change?
Exit Criteria: Clear data on what's working. Optimized campaigns. 90-day roadmap based on real numbers.

The Joyo DNA — Message System

These are the messages that build Joyo's reputation, not just visibility. They answer: 'Why should I care about this burger place?' Intentional storytelling about the philosophy, the craft, and the people behind the counter.

MESSAGE 01

"We Grind Our Own Beef. Every. Single. Day."

Fresh ground daily vs. frozen warehouse patties. Close-up grinding video, behind-the-scenes photo series, Reel with voiceover.

MESSAGE 02

"You Can See Everything That Goes Into Your Burger."

Halal, dedicated GF fryer, nothing hidden. Transparency focus — ingredient lineup photo, GF fryer explainer, "what's in your burger" video.

MESSAGE 03

"Meet the People Who Make Your Burger."

Real people, real pride. The team IS the brand. Team intro Reels, day-in-the-life, "Ask the crew" series.

MESSAGE 04

"Joy" — The Philosophy

The name means something. Food that brings happiness. Simple done exceptionally well. Wassim's origin story, customer reaction videos, "what does joy taste like?"

MESSAGE 05

"Hand-Cut Quebec Fries."

Not from a bag. Hand-cut from Quebec potatoes every day. Satisfying cutting video, potato-to-plate journey.

MESSAGE 06

"Your Neighborhood Burger Spot."

Two locations rooted in Rosemont and Verdun. Neighborhood walkup shots, regulars, street-level feel.
