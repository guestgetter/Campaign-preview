The Conversion Gap

Reservations are up 7.3% over the last 90 days (3,637 → 3,901). But actual covers dropped 8.1% (12,666 → 11,646). The marketing is driving more demand — roughly 1,020 covers are getting lost to cancellations and no-shows downstream.

Revenue Opportunity
At $85 average per cover, 1,020 lost covers represents roughly $87K in leaked revenue over 90 days. A pre-visit confirmation system (3 days out + day-of) recaptures a meaningful share by either locking in attendance or freeing the table for someone who will show.

What This Means
We matched every Meta lead by email, phone, and name against the full OpenTable guest database. 224 of 957 leads booked a table. 111 actually showed up and ate — generating $19,599 in tracked revenue at $30 per seated guest. These guests average 1.7 visits each, so the ads aren't just filling a seat once. The 23.4% match rate is a floor — the real number influenced by Meta is higher.
Discover Campaign Is the Engine
731 leads across both Discover variants, 92 who visited, $14,918 in revenue. At roughly $1.84 CPL, this campaign is producing seated guests at an extraordinary cost — under $15 per person who walks through the door. Chef's Table continues to stand out as the group dining format that actually converts interest into bookings.

What This Means
Google is showing Carbon Bar to more people across a wider range of restaurant searches — impressions up 11% with average position improving. The CTR dip is expected: you're now showing up for broader, discovery-based queries (people searching "BBQ Toronto" rather than "Carbon Bar"). This is how organic growth works — visibility first, then we optimize click-through as these new audiences become familiar.
Milestone
Full structured data layer deployed across the entire site — menu schema, local business, and organization markup. Google's Rich Results Test confirms 4 valid items. Carbon Bar has moved from limited machine-readability to a fully structured entity. This is infrastructure that compounds.
The Compounding Picture

Every period, the system gets smarter. Negative keywords sharpen Google Ads efficiency. Structured data deepens how search engines understand the restaurant. Lead campaigns build an owned audience you keep forever. This isn't just marketing spend — it's accumulated intelligence that makes every future dollar work harder.
