OpenTable Optimization — Quick Wins

We audited Lucie's OpenTable setup. The infrastructure is strong — Premium plan, SMS confirmation and reminders enabled, waitlist SMS active, direct messaging templates built. Here's where we can tighten it up to close the reservation-to-visit gap.

No-Show Prevention
Credit card hold is available but not enabled. Turning on a $25–50/person hold in OpenTable settings is the single fastest way to reduce no-shows. People who put a card down show up.
Confirmation & Reminder SMS
Both are active but using generic copy. The confirmation SMS opens with "we don't offer à la carte" — should lead with a warm welcome. The reminder SMS is functional but could build excitement for the meal. Rewriting both to sound like Lucie takes 10 minutes and changes the guest's first impression.
Transactional Email & Response Messages
Confirmation emails have no branding — no logo, no colors, no welcome line. The response time message says "staff will respond when available." Both are easy fixes that make every automated touchpoint feel like Lucie instead of a generic system.
Untapped: Email Marketing
Lucie has a full guest database in OpenTable but has never sent a marketing email through it. Three ready-to-launch campaigns: re-engagement for lapsed guests (60–90 days), birthday/anniversary automation, and a private dining upsell. This is owned audience — zero ad spend required.
